Game Day: Observations at the Jeff Ishbia Field at McLane Stadium

By: Tyler Burt

Event: MSU vs Ohio State | Ticket: free with student id | Date: March 21, 2025

I attended the MSU vs Ohio State softball game on March 21, 2025 at The Jeff Ishbia Field at McLane Stadium. It was a chilly and windy afternoon but the sun provided some warmth during the game. I focus on race and ethnicity, corporatism, and gender. To do this I took notes on language, interactions, traditions, and broader social themes.

Social Issue 1: Race and ethnicity

In chapter 9 of Sports in Society: Issues and Controversies, Race and Ethnicity, Coakely remarks that the composition of a team may be homogeneous, but the leadership can reveal deeper radical dynamics. Knowing this while the players on both teams are predominantly white the coaches being individuals of color means there might be a deeper racial dynamic.

Social Issue 2: Class and Corporatism

At the game, traditions underscored the commercial focus of MSU athletics, from sponsor acknowledgments at inning changes to a t-shirt toss featuring merchandise and gift cards, as well as foul ball and home run sponsorships. Concession prices were high without MSU combo discounts, and premium seating near home plate appeared reserved for family and friends.

Social Issue 3: Gender

The women’s softball scene is substantially underseen and heard of while men’s baseball is thriving reflecting gender biases, whether it's intentional or not this reflects on society. Despite men’s softball leagues, stereotypes persist and men consider softball to be a purely female sport. Continuing there is a significant pay gap between the NPF and MLB players which underscores systemic inequalities.

Does Sport Reflect Society?

Sports intentionally or not serve as a microcosm of society or reflect that society's beliefs. Which reveals many underlying issues such as racial, gender, and economic dynamics and inequality. Although the player base is predominantly white, diverse coaching suggests more complex racial power structures. Significant sponsorship and underlying commercial influences.While the gender disparities in professional opportunities highlight the inequality and opinion of society. Overall, the game encapsulates existing societal issues.
